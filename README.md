# 🎙️ Apex PR: Aria Lim Negotiation Simulation

**Apex** is an AI-powered negotiation trainer designed to help students improve their negotiation skills. It was developed as part of [UTC2120](https://nusmods.com/courses/UTC2120/the-art-and-science-of-negotiation) *The Art and Science of Negotiation* at NUS.

Users play the role of a Senior Account Director at **Apex PR** to negotiate representation terms with **Aria Lim**, a high-profile influencer facing a potentially career-ending controversy.

Try **Apex** here: https://apex-pr-negotiation.onrender.com/

---

## 📖 Case Overview
* **The Client:** Aria Lim (1.8M Followers).
* **The Crisis:** Alleged romantic scandal with a married public figure; $250k skincare deal at risk.
* **The Deadline:** 48-hour window to present a strategy to the brand.
* **The Goal:** Reach a formal agreement on fees, disclosure levels, and crisis strategy while navigating Aria's defensive persona and ethical boundaries.

---

## 🛠️ Key Features

### 1. Dynamic Persona Engine
Aria reacts in real-time to the user's negotiation style. Using "Information Asymmetry" logic, she only reveals her "Hidden ZOPA" (Zone of Possible Agreement) and the truth behind the controversy if the user builds sufficient trust and asks precise questions.

### 2. State-Aware Interaction
* **Turn Counter:** The simulation tracks message length. Aria becomes increasingly anxious as the 48-hour deadline approaches (Turn 15+).
* **Ethical Guardrails:** The system is programmed to reject proposals involving deception or evidence suppression, testing the user's professional integrity.

### 3. Professional UI/UX
* **Auto-Expanding Interface:** Optimized for long-form drafting of professional PR proposals.
* **Aesthetic Theme:** A "Rose Gold" visual language reflecting the lifestyle influencer domain.

### 4. Automated Performance Analytics
Upon reaching a deal or a walk-away condition, the AI generates a comprehensive analysis using:
* **Thomas-Kilmann Conflict Mode Instrument (TKI):** Identifying the user's primary conflict style (e.g., Collaborating vs. Competing).
* **Harvard’s 7 Elements of Negotiation:** Evaluating Interests, Options, and Legitimacy.
